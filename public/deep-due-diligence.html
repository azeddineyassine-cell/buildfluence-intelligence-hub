Investigation timeline

D+3 · WEEK 1
Initial analysis · open-source decoding

Review of institutional websites. The primary target (DELAWARE) lists a single executive. But a cross-reference in a third-party source mentions a systematic co-investor: a fund based in another offshore state (NEVADA).

Deviation flagged
D+10 · WEEK 2
Mapping of executives

NEVADA's official site lists 3 managers. A deeper investigation uncovers a fourth, a former banker, active in investor relations, never mentioned in public communications. Sign of asymmetric governance.

Shadow executive identified
D+20 · WEEK 3
Historical lineage of the President

The President of NEVADA previously chaired a former agro-food group (OHIO) liquidated for fraud in 2017. His predecessor is incarcerated. He himself is not implicated, but it is with OHIO's funds that he capitalized NEVADA.

Red signal
D+28 · WEEK 4
ICIJ · Offshore Leaks query

Search in the International Consortium of Investigative Journalists database. The President of NEVADA owns 2 offshore companies domiciled in the British Virgin Islands, sharing the same Moscow address — nationality confirmed.

Material evidence
D+35 · WEEK 5
Compliance & sanctions analysis

None of the entities or individuals appear on UN, OFAC, EU or Interpol sanctions lists. The risk is not regulatory — it is reputational and structural.

Compliance: clean
D+42 · WEEK 6
Final delivery & recommendation

48-page dossier + dashboard. Recommendation: Go on the operating principal (DELAWARE), Caution on the co-investor (NEVADA) with reinforced protective clauses. Partnership signed, on a redefined scope.

Mission accomplished
