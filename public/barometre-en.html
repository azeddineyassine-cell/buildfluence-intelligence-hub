A Strategic-Driven Approach

Five analytical axes combined with Economic Intelligence, AI, Big Data, and human intelligence.

Sources & Collection

Period: 2025/07/14 to 2025/08/14. Language: English and French. Sources: Web, social networks, institutional websites, press, LinkedIn, broadcast, blogs. 24,600 pieces of data collected. Reliability guaranteed via NewsGuard Index and OSINT Fact-Checking module.

Axis 1: Global Investment

Daily monitoring of investment trends in competing countries across 9 strategic sectors. Objective: detect activity peaks, understand triggers, measure investor interest. Deliverable: Operational mapping of key players, new entrants, and emerging sectoral signals.

Axis 2: Investment by Sector

Identify priority sectors among competitors. Locate attractive hubs and understand their drivers. Deliverable: Sectoral distribution rate + Comparative scoring by competitor country.

Axis 3 & 4: By Countries & Sectors by Competitors

Competitive analysis of key competitiveness sectors. Highlight the sectoral strengths of each competitor. Deliverable: Ranking of leading sectors generating attractiveness + High criticality competitive trends and alerts.

Axis 5: Morocco's Positioning

Evaluate Morocco's sectoral performance against each competitor. Exhaustive comparison and strategic ranking. Deliverable: Top 5 competitor rankings with identification of the 3 dominant sectors per country.

The Modern Attractiveness Equation

Strong convergence between "Countries" + "Investment" and "Investment Opportunities". Investors seek a country before a sector.

  • 02

  • 03

South Africa: Heavy Arsenal, Fragile in Execution

11 dedicated structures, highest volume (9,968 pts). But institutional complexity generates counterproductive friction.

9,968Index Points · Rank 1
  • InvestSA: National one-stop-shop, FDI facilitation, aftercare, pipeline. 203 cumulative projects (ZAR 1.8 trillion).
  • Butterfly Strategy (August 2025): Response to US tariffs (30%). 4 pillars: Market Diversification, Multi-export Channels, Agility, Resilience.
  • BLSA Reform Tracker: 240 reforms publicly tracked. 65% on track, 88% economic reforms underway.
  • Structured SEZs: Coega SEZ (~R12bn), Dube TradePort (40+ projects), Invest Cape Town (744 projects, cumulative R114bn).
Critical Limitations

Overlap of roles among 11 structures. Lack of hierarchical clarity. Confusion and fragmentation. Systemic delays and inefficiencies despite the arsenal.
